Santa Claus

Santa Claus' tales started from a real person. In the 4th century, a bishop named St. Nicholas lived in the Lycian port of Myra. According to the legends, St. Nicholas was entitled a gift-giver because he helped a man who could not afford the dowries for her three daughters. When night came, St. Nicholas secretly threw three bags of gold on the man's window. And so, the practice of giving gifts have been passed on up until today.
